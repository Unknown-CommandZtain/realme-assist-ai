Always-On-Display (AOD) Be aware that the following explanations are simplified. Why don't I have an Always-On-Display? If your device has an LCD panel, AODs are pointless. The entire backlight of an LCD must remain on to show even a single pixel, which would drain your battery rapidly. AODs are strictly designed for AMOLED displays, where individual black pixels can physically turn off to save power. Why can't I customize my AOD? This is usually due to hardware limitations on budget or mid-range devices, often referred to as having a "ram-less display." This means the display relies entirely on the system RAM rather than having its own dedicated memory, limiting its standby rendering capabilities. Full AOD customization is generally reserved for premium panels found on modern flagship series (like the GT 5, GT 6, GT 7 Pro, and numbered Pro+ series).
